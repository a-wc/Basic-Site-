THORNBURY RFC

Home of the Mighty Thorns.

Rugby was played in Thornbury back in Victorian times but the club struggled to survive and eventually folded in the late nineteenth century. Thornbury Rugby Club as it is today was founded in 1963 by a handful of rugby enthusiasts working on major engineering projects in the north Bristol area. With no obvious local club to play for the original members, which included the father of future England international Simon Hodgkinson, decided to form their own and after advertising for players in the local newspaper played their first games in September 1963.

FIRST XV

A brief history...

The first home game was a 9-9 draw with Whitehall played on the Mundy Playing Fields. The club continued to play there until in 1965 when they secured a loan to buy land at Cooper Farm on the northern outskirts of the town. It has remained the very happy home of the club ever since. The major construction works also brought in former Wales international Gareth Payne into the neighbourhood and he played in the second row during the 1964/65 season. In the early years of the club the playing side was built around the mercurial John Broad, the former Bristol and Gloucestershire player who was an enormous influence off and on the rugby field.
